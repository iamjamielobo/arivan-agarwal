My societal contribution

Last year when I was at a wedding in my home-town in Jodhpur, I happened to visit some of the villages by sheer chance with an uncle. Being a complete city boy, what I witnessed in the village moved me beyond comprehension. I saw the lack of sanitation and facilities in basic amenities like toilets which made it impossible for people to use it even though they had provision for a toilet.

I took on the task to research and discover the challenges faced by the villagers to live a life of dignity. My campaign is in the area of Sanitation, which is an important aspect of this basic right. I conducted a small pilot project addressing two issues: 1. Usage by the specially-abled people: Standard Indian style toilet seat is not accessible/convenient for them to sit and 2. providing lighting solutions in public toilets: Making them usable during the night.

After conducting many talks and presentations, I was able to convey my idea to many people in the big cities to come forward to contribute to my cause and was able to collect a total of Rs.3,84,512 through a crowdfunding platform. I collaborated with an IIT alumni to find a solution for the old and disabled. With the money collected I was able to provide 50 RETROFIT TOILET KITS and 50 LED Lights in small rural area Jodhpur in Rajasthan. I personally went there and documented the work and saw that the people were quite satisfied and happy. I am in touch with a few agencies who can take this project up on a larger scale. I also presented the benefits of improved sanitation in people's lives to a company Aviom Housing. They liked my thoughts and deployed money and resources to set up new toilets in rural parts of Rajasthan.

I also partnered with an NGO Masoom and taught underprivileged students Computer Science, English language skills and Mathematics.
